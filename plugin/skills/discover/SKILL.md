# /pandacorp:discover

Opportunity discovery. `$ARGUMENTS` can narrow the niche (e.g.: "/pandacorp:discover tools for teachers"); without arguments, it explores broadly, guided by the profile.

**Open scope.** It is not limited to "monetizable apps": an opportunity can be solved with a web or mobile app, a Claude Code tool or tooling, a prompt or prompt system, an automation, a rework, etc. What matters is **a real problem with good return**.

**Return is NOT just money.** The **opportunity** (reach, contacts, positioning), learning and personal value also count. A modest monetization idea can be worth a lot if it leverages an owner's lever or opens doors.

## Step 0 — Read the profile

Read `factory/profile.md` (generated by `/pandacorp:onboarding`). Extract interests, hobbies, likes/dislikes, goals, **assets/levers** (audience, community, network, niche, skills, data), monetization appetite and preferred project types. If the profile doesn't exist, say so and suggest running onboarding; you can continue only with the general stream (below) until there is a profile.

## Steps

1. **Launch `researcher` in parallel, in TWO streams (aim for ~50/50):**

   **Stream A — General opportunities (topic-independent).** The best of the moment by value/ease/return, whether or not the owner likes them:
   - Complaints and pain points on Reddit (r/smallbusiness, r/productivity, niche subreddits) and forums.
   - Negative reviews of existing apps/tools (what users ask for and nobody provides).
   - Trends and "alternatives to X" / "tools for Y" (unmet demand), including gaps that AI opened up.

   **Stream B — Aligned with the profile.** Ideas that fit the owner's interests/goals **or that leverage their assets/open doors for them**, even if the monetary return is smaller:
   - Pain points within their interests/hobbies/niche.
   - Ideas that leverage their assets (e.g. if they have an audience or community: something that audience would use, or that boosts their positioning).
   - Tools that would solve their own life/work.

   Each agent brings opportunities with **EVIDENCE** (links to real expressions of the pain/demand), not opinions from articles.

2. **Filter** with Pandacorp criteria: implementable by one person in weeks (not months) with the golden paths or as tooling/prompt/automation; without heavy regulatory requirements (health, regulated finance); and with **clear return** — monetary **or** of opportunity/value to the owner.

3. **Deduplicate against the existing base**: read the frontmatter of `factory/ideas/*.md`; if the opportunity is already there, add the new evidence to that card instead of duplicating.

4. **Document the best ones** (aim for ~3-5 per stream) as cards (`factory/ideas/<slug>.md`, `_idea-template.md` format, `origin: discovery`, `status: documented`) with score and rationale. Fill in `project_type`, `profile_alignment` and `return_type`. The interesting discards: list them in the summary without creating a card.

5. **Report** in two blocks —**General** and **Aligned with you**— each with a table (title, project type, score, return type, difficulty) and your recommendation. In the aligned ones, explain *why* they fit (which interest/asset they leverage).

## Rules
- Evidence first: each card carries ≥2 links to real expressions of the pain/demand.
- Don't inflate scores: a 70+ must be genuinely promising (see the rubric in `/pandacorp:new-idea`).
- Honor the dual criterion: do NOT discard an aligned idea just because its monetization is low, nor a brilliant general idea just because it is not "on the owner's topic".
- It runs on demand today; it is designed to also be run as a scheduled job (without human interaction) — in that case it only reports and records.
